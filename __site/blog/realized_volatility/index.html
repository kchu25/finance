Realized Volatility: Complete Mathematical Explanation

What is Realized Volatility?

Realized Volatility (RV) measures the actual historical volatility of a stock based on observed price movements. It quantifies how much the stock price has fluctuated over a given period.

Step-by-Step Calculation

Step 1: Calculate Daily Returns

Given a sequence of closing prices P0,P1,P2,…,PnP_0, P_1, P_2, \ldots, P_nP0​,P1​,P2​,…,Pn​ over nnn days:

ri=ln⁡(PiPi−1)=ln⁡(Pi)−ln⁡(Pi−1)r_i = \ln\left(\frac{P_i}{P_{i-1}}\right) = \ln(P_i) - \ln(P_{i-1})ri​=ln(Pi−1​Pi​​)=ln(Pi​)−ln(Pi−1​)

Variables:

  • PiP_iPi​ = closing price on day iii

  • Pi−1P_{i-1}Pi−1​ = closing price on the previous day

  • rir_iri​ = log return (continuous return) on day iii

  • ln⁡\lnln = natural logarithm

Why log returns?

  • They are additive: r1→3=r1+r2+r3r_{1\to 3} = r_1 + r_2 + r_3r1→3​=r1​+r2​+r3​

  • More theoretically sound for statistical analysis

  • Assumption: returns follow a normal distribution (in Black-Scholes framework)

Example:

  • Day 1: P0=$100P_0 = \$100P0​=$100, Day 2: P1=$102P_1 = \$102P1​=$102

  • r1=ln⁡(102/100)=ln⁡(1.02)≈0.0198r_1 = \ln(102/100) = \ln(1.02) \approx 0.0198r1​=ln(102/100)=ln(1.02)≈0.0198 or 1.98%

Step 2: Calculate Standard Deviation of Daily Returns

σdaily=1n−1∑i=1n(ri−rˉ)2\sigma_{\text{daily}} = \sqrt{\frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{i=1}^{n} (r_i - \bar{r})^2}σdaily​=n−11​i=1∑n​(ri​−rˉ)2​

Variables:

  • nnn = number of days in the sample period

  • rir_iri​ = daily return on day iii

  • rˉ\bar{r}rˉ = mean (average) of all daily returns = 1n∑i=1nri\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n} r_in1​∑i=1n​ri​

  • σdaily\sigma_{\text{daily}}σdaily​ = standard deviation of daily returns

  • (n−1)(n-1)(n−1) = Bessel's correction (unbiased estimator for sample standard deviation)

Interpretation: This measures the typical day-to-day fluctuation in returns.

Example:

  • If daily returns are: 1%, -0.5%, 2%, 0.5%, -1%

  • Calculate mean, then compute deviations from mean, square them, average, and take square root

Step 3: Annualize the Daily Volatility

σannual=σdaily×252\sigma_{\text{annual}} = \sigma_{\text{daily}} \times \sqrt{252}σannual​=σdaily​×252​

Variables:

  • σannual\sigma_{\text{annual}}σannual​ = annualized volatility (the final realized volatility)

  • σdaily\sigma_{\text{daily}}σdaily​ = daily standard deviation from Step 2

  • 252252252 = typical number of trading days per year (excludes weekends and holidays)

  • 252≈15.87\sqrt{252} \approx 15.87252​≈15.87

Why multiply by 252\sqrt{252}252​?

This comes from the statistical property that variance scales linearly with time for independent random variables.

Side note: If the daily variance is σ2\sigma^2σ2, then over TTT days, the total variance is T×σ2T \times \sigma^2T×σ2. This means that if you double the time period, the variance doubles (not the standard deviation). The standard deviation grows with T\sqrt{T}T​, which is why we multiply by 252\sqrt{252}252​ to annualize daily volatility.

Proof: Let r1,r2,…,rTr_1, r_2, \ldots, r_Tr1​,r2​,…,rT​ be independent daily returns, each with variance Var⁡(ri)=σ2\operatorname{Var}(r_i) = \sigma^2Var(ri​)=σ2. The variance of the sum is:

>Var⁡(∑i=1Tri)=∑i=1TVar⁡(ri)=Tσ2> > \operatorname{Var}\left(\sum_{i=1}^T r_i\right) = \sum_{i=1}^T \operatorname{Var}(r_i) = T \sigma^2 > >Var(i=1∑T​ri​)=i=1∑T​Var(ri​)=Tσ2>

because the variance of the sum of independent variables is the sum of their variances.

Mathematical Derivation:

Assume daily returns rir_iri​ are independent and identically distributed (i.i.d.) with variance σdaily2\sigma_{\text{daily}}^2σdaily2​.

Over TTT days, the cumulative return is:

RT=r1+r2+⋯+rTR_T = r_1 + r_2 + \cdots + r_TRT​=r1​+r2​+⋯+rT​

The variance of the sum of independent random variables is:

Var(RT)=Var(r1)+Var(r2)+⋯+Var(rT)=T×σdaily2\text{Var}(R_T) = \text{Var}(r_1) + \text{Var}(r_2) + \cdots + \text{Var}(r_T) = T \times \sigma_{\text{daily}}^2Var(RT​)=Var(r1​)+Var(r2​)+⋯+Var(rT​)=T×σdaily2​

Therefore, the standard deviation over TTT days is:

σT=Var(RT)=T×σdaily2=T×σdaily\sigma_T = \sqrt{\text{Var}(R_T)} = \sqrt{T \times \sigma_{\text{daily}}^2} = \sqrt{T} \times \sigma_{\text{daily}}σT​=Var(RT​)​=T×σdaily2​​=T​×σdaily​

For one year (T=252T = 252T=252 trading days):

σannual=252×σdaily\sigma_{\text{annual}} = \sqrt{252} \times \sigma_{\text{daily}}σannual​=252​×σdaily​

Intuition: Volatility grows with the square root of time, not linearly. A stock with 2% daily volatility has roughly 2%×15.87≈31.7%2\% \times 15.87 \approx 31.7\%2%×15.87≈31.7% annual volatility, not 2%×252=504%2\% \times 252 = 504\%2%×252=504%.

Complete Formula

Putting it all together:

σRV=252×1n−1∑i=1n(ln⁡PiPi−1−rˉ)2\boxed{\sigma_{RV} = \sqrt{252} \times \sqrt{\frac{1}{n-1} \sum_{i=1}^{n} \left(\ln\frac{P_i}{P_{i-1}} - \bar{r}\right)^2}}σRV​=252​×n−11​i=1∑n​(lnPi−1​Pi​​−rˉ)2​​

Where:

  • σRV\sigma_{RV}σRV​ = Realized Volatility (annualized)

  • PiP_iPi​ = closing price on day iii

  • nnn = number of days in sample period

  • rˉ\bar{r}rˉ = mean of daily log returns

  • 252252252 = trading days per year

Practical Example

Given: 5 days of closing prices: 100,102,101,103,102100, 102, 101, 103, 102100,102,101,103,102

Step 1: Calculate daily returns

  • r1=ln⁡(102/100)=0.0198r_1 = \ln(102/100) = 0.0198r1​=ln(102/100)=0.0198

  • r2=ln⁡(101/102)=−0.0099r_2 = \ln(101/102) = -0.0099r2​=ln(101/102)=−0.0099

  • r3=ln⁡(103/101)=0.0196r_3 = \ln(103/101) = 0.0196r3​=ln(103/101)=0.0196

  • r4=ln⁡(102/103)=−0.0098r_4 = \ln(102/103) = -0.0098r4​=ln(102/103)=−0.0098

Step 2: Calculate mean return

rˉ=0.0198−0.0099+0.0196−0.00984=0.0049\bar{r} = \frac{0.0198 - 0.0099 + 0.0196 - 0.0098}{4} = 0.0049rˉ=40.0198−0.0099+0.0196−0.0098​=0.0049

Step 3: Calculate daily standard deviation

σdaily=(0.0198−0.0049)2+(−0.0099−0.0049)2+(0.0196−0.0049)2+(−0.0098−0.0049)23\sigma_{\text{daily}} = \sqrt{\frac{(0.0198-0.0049)^2 + (-0.0099-0.0049)^2 + (0.0196-0.0049)^2 + (-0.0098-0.0049)^2}{3}}σdaily​=3(0.0198−0.0049)2+(−0.0099−0.0049)2+(0.0196−0.0049)2+(−0.0098−0.0049)2​​ ≈0.0164 or 1.64%\approx 0.0164 \text{ or } 1.64\%≈0.0164 or 1.64%

Step 4: Annualize

σRV=0.0164×252≈0.0164×15.87≈0.26 or 26%\sigma_{RV} = 0.0164 \times \sqrt{252} \approx 0.0164 \times 15.87 \approx 0.26 \text{ or } 26\%σRV​=0.0164×252​≈0.0164×15.87≈0.26 or 26%

Result: The realized volatility is approximately 26% annualized.

Key Properties

  1. Lookback period matters: Typically use 20-252 days

    • 60 days ≈ 3 months (medium-term)
