If you don't have time to read the [Bretton Woods deep dive](/blog/US_economy/bretton_woods/), its [math companion](/blog/US_economy/bretton_woods_math/), the [velocity analysis](/blog/US_economy/velocity_gap/), or the [Chainlink logical terminus](/blog/chainlink/logical_terminus/), here's the whole thing.

## The Problem

The US has \$38 trillion in debt (~130% of GDP). Annual interest payments exceed \$1 trillion. \$15 trillion in pandemic-era debt refinances over 2026–2028 at much higher rates. Traditional Treasury buyers — China, Japan, foreign central banks — are leaving. The Fed is tightening.

The US needs new, large-scale, *mandatory* buyers of Treasuries.

## The Pattern

The US has solved this exact problem three times in 80 years, with the same strategy each time: **build a system where participating in the global economy requires holding dollar-denominated assets.**

**Bretton Woods (1944–1971):** Peg the dollar to gold. Peg all currencies to the dollar. Every country needs dollar reserves to maintain their peg → reserves held as Treasuries. Structural demand scales with world trade.

**Petrodollar (1974–present):** Price oil in dollars. Every country needs dollars to buy energy → oil exporters recycle surplus into Treasuries. Structural demand scales with the global oil bill.

**GENIUS Act (2025–present):** Require stablecoins to be 100% backed by Treasuries. Every stablecoin minted forces a Treasury purchase. Structural demand scales with the on-chain economy.

One equation describes all three:

$$D_{\text{Treasury}} = \mu \cdot A(t)$$

Treasury demand ($D$) equals a demand coefficient ($\mu$) times an adoption variable ($A$) — world trade, oil consumption, or stablecoin supply. Linear. Simple. [Proven three times](/blog/US_economy/bretton_woods_math/).

## What's Different This Time

Two things.

**1. The demand coefficient is fixed by law.** Under Bretton Woods, countries *chose* how much reserves to hold. Under the petrodollar system, OPEC *chose* to invest in Treasuries. Under the GENIUS Act, stablecoin issuers are *legally required* to hold Treasuries. No choice. No behavioral uncertainty. $\mu$ went from voluntary → conventional → mandatory.

**2. Digital velocity.** Bretton Woods operated on a cycle of months to years (gold shipments, diplomatic cables). Petrodollar recycling cycled quarterly. Stablecoin issuance cycles in *hours*. The [velocity gap](/blog/US_economy/velocity_gap/) means each dollar of stablecoin supply generates ~200x more Treasury market activity than each dollar of Bretton Woods reserves. \$2T in stablecoins does the work of \$400T in old-system reserves.
